Genesis

KRIBHCO was one of the first Plants to come up in Hazira Area in the State of Gujarat, on the bank of river Tapti, 15 km from Surat cityand is a leading Fertilizer player in the Cooperative Sector.


KRIBHCO was incorporated as a national level Multi State Cooperative Society to implement first gas based “state-of-the-art” high capacity Fertilizer Complex consisting of 2 x 1350 MTPD Ammonia plants and 4 x 1100 MTPD Urea plants each with annual installed capacity of 8.91 Lakh MT of Ammonia and 14.52 Lakh MT of Urea

Milestones

17th April,1980

Incorporation

KRIBHCO was incorporated on 17th April 1980 as a national level Multi State Cooperative Society to implement first gas based “state-of-the-art” high capacity Fertilizer Complex consisting of 2 x 1350 MTPD Ammonia plants and 4 x 1100 MTPD Urea plants each with annual installed capacity of 8.91 Lakh MT of Ammonia and 14.52 Lakh MT of Urea.

5th February ,1982

Laying of Foundation Stone

Late Smt. Indira Gandhi, former Prime Minister of India laid the Foundation Stone on February 5, 1982.KRIBHCO has setup a Fertilizer Complex to manufacture Urea, Ammonia & Bio-fertilizers at Hazira in the State of Gujarat, on the bank of river Tapti, 15 Kms from Surat city on Surat – Hazira State Highway.

26th November ,1985

Trial Production

The trial production commenced from November, 1985 .Hazira Fertilizer complex was commissioned in 1985 in record time with saving of Rs. 90 crore in project cost.

1st March 1986

Commercial Production

Within a very short time of 3 months, the commercial production commenced from March 01, 1986.

2000

Revision of Installed Capacity

Capacities of Ammonia & Urea plants were reassessed by Govt. of India to 2 x 1520 MTPD Ammonia and 4 x 1310 MTPD Urea with revised annual installed capacity of 10.03 Lakh MT of Ammonia and 17.29 Lakh MT of Urea.

2012

Plant Revamp

The Hazira Fertilizer Complex was subsequently revamped to increase its production capacity by 4.65 LMTPA of Urea. After the revamp, the rated capacity has been enhanced to 2 x 3325 MTPD Urea and 2 x 1890 MTPD Ammonia with revamped annual capacity of 12.47 Lakh MT of Ammonia and 21.95 Lakh MT of Urea
